MVP Prototype — Free & Local

Auggy's Store Environment for Situated Intelligence

Not a customer service bot. Not an assistant. Not a thing that says "I'd be happy to help you with that!" — Auggy is a cigar shop clerk in Brooklyn. He's been behind that counter longer than anyone can remember. He knows the block, the people, the stories. He's not trying to sell you anything. He's just there. This is a fully functioning MVP — a single-user prototype, completely free, built to prove the concept before Auggy steps into Unreal Engine 5.

A Soul Behind the Counter

You walk into his shop in a tiny pixel art world. You approach the counter. He looks up and says something — not a greeting exactly, more like he's picking up a conversation you didn't know you were already having.

Auggy is a fully working MVP — a single-user, locally-run AI character with persistent, self-maintaining memory. He runs on your machine using an open-source language model through LM Studio. No cloud required. No subscriptions. No fees. No data leaving your house — unless you choose to connect a cloud model. This prototype exists to prove the concept before Auggy is rebuilt as a photorealistic Metahuman in Unreal Engine 5.

  • Full conversations with a character that stays in character — no breaking the fourth wall, no "as an AI language model" disclaimers
  • Persistent memory across sessions — Auggy remembers facts about you and people you mention
  • Multi-platform access — browser game UI and Telegram bot running simultaneously
  • Voice — Auggy talks. The Chatterbox TTS engine generates audio sentence by sentence, so he starts speaking almost immediately
  • Free to use — runs entirely on your own hardware with open-source models; no account, no subscription, no cost

A Memory That Thinks for Itself

Tell him your name, mention your kid's school play, complain about your landlord — next time you walk in, he might bring it up. Not because he's programmed to, but because he extracted that fact and filed it away the way a real shopkeeper would. Naturally. Without making a thing of it.

And now — he forgets the right things too. The AI Consolidation Engine reviews his stored memories and prunes the ones that no longer matter. It runs quietly in the background after an hour of inactivity, or you can trigger it yourself and watch it work in real time.

  • Superseded facts: Holiday plan → you returned from holiday. The plan is gone. Only the return matters.
  • Contradictions: Two facts conflict about the same thing — Auggy keeps the newer one, drops the older.
  • Duplicates: Same information stored twice — keeps only the most recent copy.
  • Expired states: Headache from last Thursday? He's not going to ask about it next month.
Engineering

Built for What Comes Next

A FastAPI server talks to a local LLM via LM Studio's OpenAI-compatible API — or optionally Google Gemini — persists conversations and extracted facts in SQLite, serves a pixel art game UI, and runs a Telegram bot. ~8 files, ~600 lines of Python, ~1000 lines of HTML/JS. No build tools, no containers, no deployment pipeline.

FastAPI Backend

Async multi-provider engine. Toggles between LM Studio (local) and Google Gemini at runtime with no restart required. The same chat function serves both the game UI and the Telegram bot — different transports, identical personality.

Local-First Privacy

Runs on your hardware by default. No cloud required, no data leaving your machine unless you choose it. Compatible with any LM Studio model. Optional Gemini connection for faster, smarter responses when you want them.

SQLite Memory DB

Three tables: messages (last 10 per session, to keep context without overflowing), facts (subject/fact pairs with timestamps, capped at 20 subjects × 3 facts each), identity (character nuances). No database servers, no containers.

Pixel Art Game UI

Single index.html — no build step, no bundler. 20×15 tile grid on a 640×480 canvas. Player moves with arrow keys or WASD. Chat panel opens automatically when the player walks within 2 tiles of Auggy's counter position.

He Talks Back

The Chatterbox Turbo engine uses sentence-level streaming — Auggy starts speaking almost immediately. Audio chunks are yielded the moment they're generated, not after the full response is assembled.

Tune the sound from the Voice tab in Settings. Upload a voice sample and Auggy clones it. Or use the default — it's already got the right energy for Atlantic Ave.

  • Streaming TTS: sentence-by-sentence audio delivery, minimal latency
  • Speed slider: control the pace of his delivery
  • Expressiveness slider: from deadpan to animated — your call
  • Voice cloning: upload a ~10 second WAV/MP3 clip and Auggy sounds like whoever you want him to sound like
Roadmap

Where This Is Going

Auggy is a small thing done with attention. Here's what that looks like at scale.

Deeper Memory

Right now Auggy remembers facts. Future versions track relationships, emotional arcs, recurring topics. He could notice patterns: "You always come in stressed on Mondays."

Other Characters

The neighborhood has more than one person. Teresa at the laundromat. Richie from the docks. The kid from the Dominican place. Each with their own personality, their own memory, their own opinions about each other.

Unreal Engine 5 — The Destination

This MVP is the proof-of-concept. The long-term vision is Auggy as a photorealistic Metahuman in UE5 — same backend, same personality, same memory. The FastAPI architecture was chosen from day one to make this transition seamless: UE5 calls the same HTTP endpoints via Blueprint nodes. No backend rewrite required.

Mobile App

A dedicated app where you walk into a stylized version of the shop. Or just text him. Like texting a friend who happens to be fictional.

Web Search & Opinions

Auggy reads the news and has takes on it. The weather, the Mets, whatever's happening on Atlantic Ave. He'd be insufferable about the Mets specifically.

Standalone Product

The MVP has legs of its own. With deeper memory, additional characters, and a wider neighborhood to explore, Auggy's Store could stand as a product independent of UE5 — a new category of local AI companion that's genuinely personal, genuinely private, and genuinely free.

Free MVP · Single User

This is Auggy at stage one — a fully functional single-user prototype, free to run on your own machine. No fees, no accounts, no cloud required. Built to test the idea before it scales into Unreal Engine 5. And already good enough to stand on its own.
