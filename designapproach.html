The 2026 Thesis

Strategy over Aesthetics.

In an era where high-fidelity visuals are generated instantly, the value of a Product Designer has shifted from production to deconstruction. My role is to ensure that what we build is grounded in structural integrity, not just aesthetic trends.

With two decades of experience, I filter every decision through the lens of long-term scalability and user retention. I do not create art; I create functional systems.

Technical
Deconstruction.

Discovery is often treated as a formality. For me, it is a surgical process. I strip products down to their core data models and user intentions to find where logic has been compromised by visual clutter or technical debt.

Decisions are never based on intuition. They are based on the documented friction between the user's mental model and the system's architecture.

Modern Specialization
The Product
Refactor.

Most SaaS products today do not need more features. They need a structural refactor. I specialize in identifying the UX debt that kills growth: bloated navigation, inconsistent mental models, and logic gaps in complex workflows.

I take existing designs and use discovery to fix the structural failures that "v2.0" visuals usually hide.

UI Architecture Audit

Mapping the disconnect between technical backends and front-end logic to eliminate user frustration.

Workflow Simplification

Reducing the cognitive load in task-heavy environments like fintech, health-tech, and enterprise dashboards.

Strategic Tooling,
Human Logic.

I use AI tools to increase efficiency and speed up the production of low-value assets. This is not a shortcut, but a way to redirect my energy toward high-level strategy and complex edge-case management that automated tools consistently ignore.

USP: The AI Auditor

Fixing Design Hallucinations

Modern UI is increasingly "hallucinated"—visually polished but functionally broken. These interfaces look impressive in a deck but fail under real-world usage because they lack human-centric logic and accessibility.

I audit these generated experiences to fix the "uncanny valley" of UI: where buttons are in the wrong place, hierarchies are illogical, and the flow feels wrong to a human user.

The Mandate

Utility is Beauty.

I do not design to make things look nice. I design to solve business problems and improve usability. If the product works perfectly, the aesthetics will follow naturally.
